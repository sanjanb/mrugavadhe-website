History & Legend

The Sacred Story of Sri Mallikarjuna Temple

The ancient Sri Mallikarjuna Temple in Mrugavadhe

The Mythological Significance

Mrugavadhe holds a special place in Hindu mythology and is deeply connected to the epic Ramayana. This sacred village is where one of the most significant events of Lord Rama's forest exile took place.

The Story of Maricha

According to the Indian Hindu scripture "Ramayana", Mrugavadhe is the place where the demon Maricha, who came in the form of a golden deer (Swarna Mriga), was slain by Lord Rama. This event was orchestrated by Ravana as part of his plan to abduct Sita Devi.

When Sita saw the beautiful golden deer, she was enchanted by its beauty and requested Rama to capture it for her. Rama, suspecting it might be a demon in disguise, still went after the deer to fulfill Sita's wish. The deer led Rama far away from their hermitage, and when Rama shot his arrow, Maricha revealed his true demonic form and cried out "Sita! Lakshmana!" in Rama's voice before dying.

Installation of the Shivalinga

After slaying the demon Maricha, Lord Rama, being deeply spiritual and following dharmic principles, installed a Shivalinga at this very spot to purify the place and seek Lord Shiva's blessings. This act transformed Mrugavadhe into a sacred pilgrimage site.

The temple that houses this sacred Shivalinga was originally named Malahanikareshwar, which later came to be known as Mallikarjuna Temple. The name "Mallikarjuna" is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and represents Lord Shiva in his supreme form.

The sacred 4.5 feet tall Shivalinga installed by Lord Rama

The Sacred Shivalinga

The most distinctive feature of Sri Mallikarjuna Temple is its magnificent 4.5 feet tall Shivalinga. This is not just any ordinary Shivalinga - it is believed to be the very one installed by Lord Rama himself over thousands of years ago.

The Shivalinga stands majestically in the sanctum sanctorum, radiating divine energy and attracting devotees from far and wide. Its imposing height and ancient craftsmanship speak volumes about the temple's historical significance and the deep devotion of its creators.

Location and Geography

Mrugavadhe is strategically located near the banks of the sacred Tunga River, adding to its spiritual ambiance. The village is situated in Thirthahalli taluk of Shivamogga district in Karnataka, approximately:

  • 26 kilometers from Thirthahalli
  • 86 kilometers from Shivamogga district headquarters
  • Part of Mrugavade Panchayat administrative area

Historical Significance

Despite being a small village with limited awareness, Mrugavadhe holds immense historical and mythological significance. The temple serves as a living testament to the events described in the Ramayana and continues to be a place where devotees come to seek blessings and spiritual solace.

The temple has been maintained through generations by devoted locals and temple authorities, preserving its ancient traditions and rituals. The peaceful environment, combined with the mythological importance, makes it a unique destination for spiritual seekers and history enthusiasts alike.
