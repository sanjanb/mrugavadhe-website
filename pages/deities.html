Sacred Deities

Divine Forms Worshipped at Our Temple

Sri Mallikarjuna

The Presiding Deity

Lord Shiva in the form of Mallikarjuna is the primary deity of our temple. The sacred 4.5 feet tall Shivalinga was installed by Lord Rama himself after slaying the demon Maricha at this very location.

Significance

  • Mallikarjuna: Lord of the Jasmine Flower
  • Height: 4.5 feet tall Shivalinga
  • Origin: Installed by Lord Rama during Treta Yuga
  • Significance: One of the sacred Jyotirlinga forms

Sacred Mantras

Main Mantra

ॐ नमः शिवाय मल्लिकार्जुन

Om Namah Shivaya Mallikarjuna

Maha Mantra

ॐ ह्रीं श्रीं मल्लिकार्जुन ज्योतिर्लिंग शिवाय नमः

Om Hreem Shreem Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga Shivaya Namah

The Sacred 4.5 feet Shivalinga of Sri Mallikarjuna

Other Sacred Deities

Sri Ganesha

Vigna Harta - Remover of Obstacles

The beloved elephant-headed deity who removes obstacles and blesses all new beginnings. Devotees first seek his blessings before offering prayers to Lord Shiva.

Special prayers: Daily at 6:00 AM & 6:00 PM

Goddess Parvati

Divine Mother - Shakti

The divine consort of Lord Shiva, representing the feminine power and creative energy of the universe. She blesses devotees with prosperity and wisdom.

Special prayers: Fridays at 7:00 PM

Sri Nandi

Vehicle & Devotee of Lord Shiva

The sacred bull who serves as Lord Shiva's mount and gatekeeper. Devotees whisper their wishes into Nandi's ear, believing he will convey them to Lord Shiva.

Accessible: During all darshan hours

Sri Hanuman

Devotee of Lord Rama

The mighty devotee of Lord Rama, protector and symbol of strength. His presence here commemorates the connection between Lord Rama and this sacred place.

Special prayers: Tuesdays & Saturdays

Sri Dakshinamurthy

The Supreme Teacher

Lord Shiva in his aspect as the divine teacher, blessing devotees with knowledge and wisdom. Students and scholars especially seek his blessings.

Special prayers: Thursdays at 5:30 PM

Navagraha

Nine Planetary Deities

The nine planetary deities who influence human destiny. Devotees perform special prayers to seek their blessings for astrological remedies and prosperity.

Special prayers: Sundays at 11:00 AM

Worship Guidelines

Prayer Sequence

  1. Begin with prayers to Lord Ganesha
  2. Offer respects to Sri Nandi
  3. Main prayers to Sri Mallikarjuna
  4. Seek blessings from other deities

Preferred Offerings

  • Bilva leaves (most sacred to Lord Shiva)
  • White flowers (jasmine, lotus)
  • Sacred thread (janeu)
  • Fruits and coconuts
  • Incense and camphor

Auspicious Days

  • Mondays: Most sacred to Lord Shiva
  • Pradosh Days: 13th day of lunar fortnight
  • Ekadashi: 11th day of lunar cycle
  • Full Moon: Purnima celebrations
  • New Moon: Amavasya rituals
